Atomic Energy as a Stable and Environmentally Friendly Source of Electricity

As part of the International Conference program, a plenary session was held focusing on the prospects of atomic energy usage in OIC countries, including the development of atomic energy in Uzbekistan. The discussion centered on the key advantages of implementing the SMR (Small Modular Reactor) project for Uzbekistan and Central Asia. These include providing a stable and environmentally friendly source of electricity, improving the country’s energy security, and creating new opportunities for economic growth. Among the speakers were Rafael Mariano Grossi, Director General of the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA); Jurabek Mirzamakhmudov, Minister of Energy of the Republic of Uzbekistan; Sama Bilbao y León, Director General of the World Nuclear Association; Azim Akhmedkhadjaev, Director of the Atomic Energy Agency under the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan; Andrey Rozhdestvin, Director General of REP Joint Stock Company; Abduvakkos Rafikov, Chairman of the Committee on Industry, Radiation, and Nuclear Safety under the Cabinet of Ministers of the Republic of Uzbekistan; and Justin Friedman, Senior Advisor at the Bureau of International Security and Nonproliferation, U.S. Department of State. The speakers highlighted the energy and climate challenges that demand global efforts to increase the share of clean energy sources, including nuclear energy. It was emphasized that for countries aspiring to sustainable development, atomic energy offers new perspectives and significant opportunities.
